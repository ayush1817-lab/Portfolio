## The problem

Commuters in Dublin rely on the TFI Live app to plan their journeys, but most tolerate it rather than trust it. Late buses, sticky timestamps, and confusing routing choices add up to an app people *check* but don't *use*.

## What I did

- Ran moderated sessions with regular commuters across three bus corridors.
- Mapped each user's mental model of "when will my bus arrive" against the app's data model.
- Identified five friction points that compound over a single journey.

## What I found

The deepest frustration wasn't inaccuracy — it was **ambiguity**. Riders couldn't tell whether "Due" meant *leaving now* or *leaving eventually*, so they stopped believing the numbers.
